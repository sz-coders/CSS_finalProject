FAQs

Any Question? Here is the Answer!

Coffee & Beans

Is espresso stronger than drip coffee?

Per ounce, espresso is more concentrated; per typical serving the total caffeine may be similar or lower depending on serving sizes.

How should I store coffee beans?

Keep beans in an airtight container, away from light, heat, and moisture. Grind just before brewing for best freshness.

Does coffee dehydrate?

Moderate coffee intake does not cause net dehydration for most people; it contributes to daily fluid intake.

Which has more caffeine — dark or light roast?

By weight light roast has slightly more caffeine, but by volume (scoops) the difference is small and usually irrelevant to most drinkers.

What is single-origin coffee?

Single-origin coffee comes from one specific region, farm, or producer, highlighting unique flavors tied to that location’s terroir.

What’s the difference between cold brew and iced coffee?

Cold brew is made by steeping coffee grounds in cold water for 12–24 hours, producing a smooth, low-acid drink.
Iced coffee is regular brewed coffee cooled and poured over ice, giving a brighter, more acidic taste.

What’s the difference between espresso and regular coffee?

Espresso is brewed under high pressure using finely ground coffee, producing a small, concentrated shot with crema on top. Regular drip or filter coffee uses gravity and a coarser grind, resulting in a larger, milder cup.

Does roast level affect caffeine content?

Not much. Light and dark roasts have almost the same caffeine per bean. Light roasts may have *slightly* more by weight because they’re denser, but the difference is minor.

How can I choose the right coffee for me?

Try different combinations! Start with your preference for flavor (light vs bold), milk (black vs creamy), and caffeine strength. Light roast Arabica for mild flavor, dark roast Robusta blends for intense kick.

Which coffee bean is stronger — Arabica, Robusta, or Liberica?

Each coffee species has its own distinct flavor and caffeine profile, so in terms of caffeine strength, Robusta is the strongest, but Liberica offers the most distinctive flavor profile.

Brewing

What is the best brewing method for beginners?

The French Press is great for beginners — it’s simple, requires no special equipment, and gives a rich, full-bodied flavor. You only need coarsely ground coffee, hot water, and 4–5 minutes of steeping time.

What is the ideal water temperature for brewing coffee?

About 90–96°C (195–205°F) is a common recommendation for most methods.

Why does my coffee taste bitter?

The ideal brewing temperature is between 90°C and 96°C (195°F–205°F). Boiling water can scorch coffee grounds, while cooler water may under-extract them.

What is grinding?

Grinding is breaking coffee beans into smaller particles to increase surface area for extraction.

How important is grind uniformity?

Very — inconsistent grind yields under- and over-extracted flavors.

Can I learn brewing visually?

Absolutely! Watch our short brewing demonstrations in the video section or explore Barista Training to see live techniques.

Coffe Around the World

Why does origin matter?

Climate, elevation, and processing shape the coffee's flavor profile — origin guides tasting expectations.

Which country drinks the most coffee per person?

Finland! Finns drink about 4 cups of coffee per day on average, making them the world’s biggest coffee consumers.

What is the rarest type of coffee?

Liberica coffee, grown mainly in the Philippines and parts of Africa, is rare and has a fruity, floral flavor unlike Arabica or Robusta.

What’s the oldest coffee-drinking culture?

Ethiopia and Yemen have the oldest coffee traditions, where coffee was brewed and shared as a social ritual centuries before it reached Europe.

How do cultures differ in their coffee habits?

In Italy, coffee is quick and social; in Turkey and Ethiopia, it’s ceremonial; in Japan, it’s artistic; and in Brazil, it’s part of everyday hospitality.

Why does origin matter?

Climate, elevation, and processing shape the coffee's flavor profile — origin guides tasting expectations.

What are heirloom varieties?

Heirloom varieties are indigenous coffee plants with unique genetic traits, often found in Ethiopia, contributing to diverse flavors.

How to explore different coffee cultures?

Visit local cafés, try traditional brewing methods, and read about regional coffee histories.

With focused practice, basic competence in espresso pulling and milk steaming is achievable in a few weeks; mastery takes months to years depending on depth of learning.
